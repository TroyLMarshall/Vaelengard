**Port Ancelin** _(also recorded as_ _**Porto Ancelini**_ _in trade ledgers)_
![[port-ancelin-crest.png]]

Founded by **Ancelin de Varnay**, a minor lord who arrived with a land grant following the Conquest, settling the sheltered bay in the 1080s. Unlike many lords who focused on inland fortifications, Ancelin saw the harbour's potential and invested heavily in quayside infrastructure, attracting sheep farmers from the surrounding hills.

Locals call themselves **Ancelins** or **Port folk**. The town's crest bears a silver ship beneath three wool bales on a field of blue. The harbour district is still known as **Varnay Quay** after his family name, and the weekly wool market — **La Foire** — retains its name to this day, a nod to the town's heritage.

By the 13th century, Port Ancelin had become a favoured stop for merchants, its deep natural harbour capable of handling the largest wool cogs of the era. The town's wealth is evident in its unusually grand church, rumoured to have been funded entirely by the wool tithe.

The town is arranged around its natural harbour, with key districts including:
- **[[Varnay Quay]]** — the bustling waterfront named after the founding family, where wool cogs are loaded
- **[[La Foire]]** — the central wool market, heart of the town's commerce
- **[[Warehouse Row]]** — where bales are stored, weighed, and inspected before export
- **[[Merchant Quarter]]** — home to the counting houses
- **[[Shearer's Ward]]** — the working district for shearing, dyeing, and tanning
- **[[St Ancelin's Church]]** — the grand wool-tithe funded centerpiece
- **[[Ancelin Hall]]** — the de Varnay family seat overlooking the town
- **[[The Fleece & Anchor]]** — the oldest tavern, on the site of Ancelin's original home
