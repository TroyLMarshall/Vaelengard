**Warehouse Row** sits between La Foire and Varnay Quay — a deliberate placement, since wool needs to move efficiently from ship or wagon, to storage, to grading, to sale, without much backtracking through town.
## **Layout**
The Row is a line of long, low timber-and-stone buildings, built wide rather than tall to keep the wool dry and accessible rather than stacked high where damp could ruin the lower bales. Most warehouses back directly onto a narrow service lane used for cart traffic, keeping the noisier work away from the main market square.
## **How Wool Moves Through**
- **Intake:** Raw fleece arrives by wagon from inland flocks, or by smaller coastal boats from outlying farms too far to cart goods by road.
- **Sorting & Baling:** Inside, wool is sorted by grade, pressed into bales, and bound — a job requiring real skill, since a poorly packed bale can shift and spoil in a ship's hold.
- **Storage:** Bales wait here for buyers, sometimes for months if a factor is holding out for a better price or waiting on a particular merchant's ship to arrive.
- **Export:** Once sold, bales are carted the short distance to Varnay Quay for loading.
## **Who Works There**
The warehouses are run by **factors** — independent wool merchants who buy from flockmasters and smallholders, store and grade the wool themselves (subject to the official Tron weighing at La Foire), and resell to foreign buyers at a markup. Beneath them work **baling hands**, **wool-sorters** (often women, valued for sharp eyes and a practiced touch in judging fiber quality), and **cart-loaders** — the lowest rung, which is where the disgraced grader Hugh Mallow now works, a detail everyone in town is quietly aware of.
## **Risk & Reward**
Warehousing wool is a gamble dressed up as a trade. Prices fluctuate with demand from Flanders and Italy, and a factor holding too much stock when prices drop can be ruined in a season — but one who reads the market right can make a fortune. This tension makes Warehouse Row one of the most socially competitive parts of town; factors watch each other's stock levels and buyer visits as closely as merchants watch ship arrivals at the quay.
## **Hazards**
Fire is the constant fear — a packed warehouse of dry wool burns fast and total, and the town has lost entire fortunes overnight in past blazes. As a result, the Row has stricter rules than most of the town: no open flame after dark, and a standing fine for anyone caught smoking a pipe near the buildings.
