**Shearer's Ward** sits at the edge of town, upwind and slightly removed from the market and harbour — partly for practical reasons (the smell of tanning and dyeing isn't welcome near La Foire) and partly because it's simply where the work has always been done, close to the routes sheep are driven in from the hill pastures.
## **The Work Itself**
The Ward handles wool at its roughest, most physical stage — everything that happens before a fleece is fit for the orderly business of grading and baling at La Foire:
- **Shearing pens** — where flocks driven down from the uplands are penned, sheared, and the raw fleece bundled for the short trip to Warehouse Row.
- **Washing troughs** — fleece is washed to remove grease, dirt, and lanolin before it's considered market-ready; the runoff has a notoriously strong smell that gives the Ward its reputation as the "downwind" district.
- **Dye works** — smaller in scale, since most wool leaves Port Ancelin undyed for Flemish weavers to finish, but a modest local dye trade exists for wool staying in the domestic cloth market.
- **Tanneries** — handling sheepskins and hides, a separate but related trade that's grown up alongside wool shearing for obvious reasons.
## **The Rhythm of the Season**
Unlike La Foire's steady weekly cycle, the Ward lives and dies by the **shearing season** — a frenzied few weeks each year when flocks come down from the hills all at once and the Ward needs far more hands than it employs the rest of the year. Seasonal shearers, often the same families returning year after year, are a familiar and welcomed sight, lodging cheaply in the Ward itself during the work.
## **Who Works There**
- **Shearers** — skilled, fast, and paid by the fleece rather than the hour, which makes speed and quality both matter enormously.
- **Washers** — grueling, low-status work, often done by the poorest in town or by the same families generation after generation.  
- **Dyers and tanners** — smaller, more settled trades with their own minor pecking order, since dye recipes and tanning methods are often closely guarded family knowledge.
## **Reputation**
The Ward has a rougher, harder-living reputation than the rest of Port Ancelin — seasonal workers, hard physical labor, and strong smells don't lend themselves to refinement. But it's also fiercely proud: without the Ward, there's no wool to grade, store, or sell, and shearers in particular carry themselves with the confidence of people who know the whole trade depends on their skill and speed.
## **Connection to Incidents**
It's worth noting Robin Attwood, the shearer at the center of the [[The Mallow Affair]] grading scandal, comes from exactly this district — his fleece passed through the Ward's pens and washing troughs before ever reaching La Foire's stalls.
