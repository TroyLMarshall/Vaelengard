### A Specialist Trade Hiding Within Several Larger Ones
"String maker" doesn't appear as a single, cleanly defined medieval guild trade the way baking or smithing does — the production of string, cord, and gut strings was distributed across several related but distinct specializations depending on the material and intended use. Let's map this properly, since the underlying craft was genuinely significant for music, archery, and countless everyday applications.
### The Key Material Distinction
String in the medieval context meant several genuinely different things depending on material:

**Plant fiber cordage**: hemp, flax, and similar plant fibers twisted into string and light cord — essentially the lighter end of the ropemaker's trade covered earlier, using the same basic twisting/laying technique but at finer scale for lighter applications

**Animal gut strings**: made from the processed intestines of sheep, cattle, or other animals — a genuinely distinct material and process producing the strings used for musical instruments and, significantly, bowstrings in some traditions

**Silk strings**: luxury instrument strings, imported silk thread twisted into fine strings for high-end instruments — a relatively rare, expensive application connecting to the luxury goods trade

**Wire strings**: drawn metal wire for some instrument applications, connecting to the metalworking trades — a later medieval development as wire-drawing technology improved
### Gut String Production — The Most Specialized Application
Animal gut string production was genuinely distinct enough from general cordage making to constitute its own specialization:

**Raw material**: the intestines of slaughtered animals — connecting directly to the butcher entry's discussion of processing the whole animal and minimizing waste, since intestines were a byproduct of normal slaughter that could be processed into a valuable product

**Processing gut into usable string**:
1. **Cleaning**: thoroughly cleaning the intestine, removing fat and other tissue
2. **Splitting**: carefully splitting the intestinal wall to produce flat strips of usable gut
3. **Soaking**: extended soaking in water (sometimes with alkali solutions) to soften and clean further
4. **Twisting while wet**: twisting multiple gut strips together while still wet and pliable
5. **Drying under tension**: allowing the twisted gut to dry while held under tension — the drying process causing the material to contract and tighten into a strong, smooth string
6. **Polishing and finishing**: smoothing the finished string

**The skilled judgment involved**: producing consistently good gut string required real practical knowledge — understanding how different animals' gut behaved, how to achieve consistent thickness and twist, and how to dry at the right tension to produce strings with the correct stiffness and playing characteristics
### Applications for Gut String
**Musical instruments**: this is the application most people think of — gut strings for lutes, harps, fiddles, and similar stringed instruments
- Connecting to the entertainer and minstrel entries, court musicians required quality strings for their instruments
- String quality directly affected instrument sound quality — a musician's performance capability depended on reliable, well-made strings
- **Regular replacement**: strings broke and needed replacing regularly, creating ongoing commercial demand

**Bowstrings**: as covered in the bowyer entry, bowstrings required specific properties — strength, resistance to moisture, and consistent stretch behavior
- **Hemp and flax for most bowstrings**: plant fiber strings were more moisture-resistant and more practically suited to the English longbow tradition specifically
- **Gut for some applications**: gut bowstrings were used in some traditions and for some bow types, particularly where their specific elastic properties suited the bow's design

**Surgical sutures**: gut's biocompatible properties made it the material for surgical sutures — connecting to the barber-surgeon and doctor/dentist entries' discussion of medieval surgery
- This represents a specialized, small-volume but genuinely important application

**Lute-string makers**: in major cultural centers where court music was significant, dedicated lute-string makers might constitute a recognized specialty — **Rome** particularly developed a reputation for quality gut string production for musical instruments
### Plant Fiber String — The Everyday Application
Beyond the specialized gut string trade, plant fiber string production served countless everyday purposes:
- **Sewing thread**: fine twisted plant fiber thread for clothing construction, connecting to the tailor entry coming shortly in this list
- **Fishing lines**: as covered in the fishermen entry
- **Various binding and tying applications**: throughout every trade in this list, string and light cordage appeared constantly in everyday practical use
- **Bookbinding**: thread for sewing manuscript gatherings together, connecting to the scribe and leatherworker entries
### Guild Structure
- String-making for everyday purposes was often absorbed into the ropemaker's trade or produced as a cottage industry byproduct of flax/hemp processing
- **Specialized gut string makers** in major centers sometimes organized into distinct guilds or were affiliated with instrument makers' guilds — the commercial music equipment trade having sufficient volume in culturally active cities to support specialization
### Worldbuilding Angle
A few fantasy-flavored hooks:
- A master gut string maker as an unexpected connection between the butcher trade and court musical culture — someone whose workshop sits in the less glamorous processing district but whose product ends up on instruments played before royalty, straddling social worlds in an interesting way
- String quality as a genuine performance variable — in a setting where a bard's magical performance depends on their instrument's string quality, a skilled string maker becomes an important NPC for musician characters, giving a genuinely mundane trade direct magical significance
- Surgical suture gut string as a specific material need — a barber-surgeon or healer character needing quality gut string for a specific medical procedure, making the string maker an unexpected quest-relevant NPC
- The gut string drying process (requiring consistent tension over extended time) as a detail explaining why a string maker's workshop has a distinctive appearance — long frames with strings stretched across them in various stages of production, an unusual and memorable visual environment for an encounter or scene
