**Margery Thorn**, called **"Old Margery"** by regulars though she's only in her late forties — the name stuck after she took over from her mother twenty years ago and the title felt permanent from the start.
## **Appearance & manner**
Broad-shouldered and weathered from decades of hauling casks, with greying red hair always tied back and forearms that have seen more burns than a blacksmith's. She has a reputation for silence that unsettles strangers and comforts regulars — she'll pour your ale without a word, but if she leans on the bar and starts talking, the whole room quiets to listen.
## **Background**
Fourth in the Thorn line to run the tavern, Margery grew up listening through the floorboards above the common room, absorbing decades of merchant gossip before she could even reach the bar. Unmarried by choice — she's said openly that she'd rather own the Fleece & Anchor outright than share it with a husband who'd want a say in running it.
## **Reputation**
Margery is trusted precisely because she's seen to forget things. Wool factors negotiate openly in her common room because they know she won't repeat what she's heard — not out of loyalty, but because she's built her entire business on being a vault. The one exception: if she catches wind of someone trying to cheat a shearer or a small farmer out of a fair price, she has a way of "remembering" that information rather loudly to the right people.
## **A personal quirk**
She keeps a ledger of her own — not of coin, but of favors owed and debts of information. It's said half the merchant quarter is in that ledger somehow, though no one's ever seen her write in it.
