### The Craft and Its Structure
A **glover** made gloves — a trade that sounds narrow but covered a surprisingly wide social and functional range, from rough working mittens for laborers to exquisitely fitted, perfumed leather gloves that were among the most prized fashion accessories a noble could own. Glovers worked primarily in **leather** (kid, lamb, deer, and finer skins for quality goods) though knitted or woven gloves also existed for cheaper, warmer, everyday use. In many cities glovers organized into their own guilds (England had glovers' guilds in multiple major towns by the 1200s-1300s), sometimes overlapping or competing with the broader leatherworkers'/tanners' trades over material sourcing and finishing rights.

**Gloves as function** — At the practical end, gloves were essential protective gear across an enormous range of trades and activities: **falconers** needed heavy, wrist-length gauntlets to handle birds of prey; **soldiers and knights** wore gauntlets (sometimes leather, sometimes leather-and-metal combined, crossing into the armorer's domain) as part of a full harness; **laborers, farmers, and smiths** needed rough protective gloves for handling tools, thorns, or hot material; and **falconry gloves specifically** deserve a mention as a genuinely specialized, high-skill sub-craft, since a badly made falconer's glove meant a badly protected wrist and hand under a raptor's talons.

**Gloves as status and symbol** — At the other end, fine leather gloves were a major **luxury fashion item** for nobility and wealthy merchants, often perfumed (scented leather gloves were a real and desired luxury good, sometimes treated with rosewater, musk, or other imported scents), dyed in fine colors, and embroidered or jeweled at the cuff. Gloves carried real **symbolic weight** in medieval custom: presenting a glove could seal a legal transaction or land transfer (a real medieval legal practice — a glove handed over as a token of an agreement), throwing down a gauntlet was a formal challenge to combat, and a lady's glove given to a knight was a recognized token of favor in courtly/chivalric custom. This gives gloves a much richer symbolic life than most trade goods — they're simultaneously mundane protective gear and a socially loaded object depending entirely on context.

**Bishops' and ceremonial gloves** — Ecclesiastical gloves (worn by bishops during Mass, richly decorated) were another prestigious specialized product, similar in spirit to the embroiderer's vestment work — a glover skilled enough to supply church or noble ceremonial gloves occupied real prestige within the trade, distinct from the maker of a farmer's rough work-mitts.

**Materials and technique** — Fine glove-making required genuinely delicate skill: cutting soft, thin leather to a precise pattern that would stretch and move with a hand's every joint without tearing or bagging, then hand-stitching seams fine enough not to chafe. This is detail-oriented, small-scale, patient work — closer in spirit to the seamstress or embroiderer's fine-motor craft than to the saddler's heavier leatherwork, even though both nominally work in leather.

**Status** — Variable by product tier, much like the whitesmith: a maker of rough work-gloves for laborers sat low in prestige and income, while a glover supplying scented, embroidered luxury gloves to nobility or ceremonial gloves to a bishop could achieve real wealth and standing, sometimes serving a single powerful patron exclusively.
## Worldbuilding Angles for D&D 2024
### Turning This Into a Background
- **Skill Proficiencies:** Sleight of Hand, Persuasion (or Insight, for reading a noble patron's taste)
- **Tool Proficiency:** Leatherworker's Tools
- **Feat:** Skilled or Fey Touched (leaning into the almost-ceremonial symbolic weight of fine gloves)
- **Equipment:** Leatherworking tools, a fine pair of unfinished gloves, a vial of scent/perfume oil, a merchant's sample case, 10 gp

This background suits a **Rogue (fine handwork, precision, sleight of hand crossover with actual sleight-of-hand skill), Bard (court gloves, symbolic favor-tokens, patron relationships), or a Ranger/falconer-adjacent character (protective gauntlet-making tied naturally to animal handling)**. The symbolic-object angle — gloves as sealed agreements, thrown challenges, tokens of favor — gives this background unusually rich roleplay hooks for a "face" or socially embedded character.
### NPC Hooks
- **The Falconer's Glover** — Specializes exclusively in falconry gauntlets, deeply connected to the hunting nobility and their birds — a great source of wilderness/hunting-adjacent rumors and a natural contact for a Ranger-type party member.
- **The Perfumer-Glover** — Makes scented luxury gloves for a noble court, quietly one of the best-informed people in the household since every noble visits to be fitted and gossip while their hands are measured.
- **The Ceremonial Glove-Maker** — Supplies a bishop or cathedral with ecclesiastical gloves, entangled in church politics and the expensive, symbolic material culture of religious ceremony.
- **The Challenge-Broker** — A glover who's quietly become the informal keeper of "thrown gauntlet" formalities in a dueling-prone town — knows every recent challenge, grudge, and unsettled honor dispute in the area.
- **The Token-Keeper** — An old glover who's kept every notable glove ever left behind, pawned, or entrusted to them as a favor-token or agreement-seal — a walking archive of the town's promises, betrothals, and broken deals.
### Game Hooks
- **The Sealed Agreement Dispute** — A land transfer or contract sealed with the old glove-exchange custom is now contested — did the glove exchange even happen, is it still binding, and can the party track down the glove (and glover who made/witnessed it) to settle the matter?
- **The Poisoned Glove** — A gift or purchased pair of fine gloves turns out to be laced with contact poison — a classic murder-mystery device, with the local glover as either the culprit, the unwitting supplier, or the investigator's best lead.
- **The Thrown Gauntlet** — A formal challenge has been issued via glove-throwing custom, and the party gets pulled in — as a champion, a second, an investigator into whether the challenge was legitimate, or someone trying to prevent the duel entirely.
- **The Falconer's Missing Glove** — A prized hunting bird has injured or lost its handler because a custom gauntlet was stolen, sabotaged, or poorly made — combines animal handling, investigation, and a bit of wilderness work.
- **The Perfumed Trail** — A distinctive, expensive glove-scent found at a crime scene or left behind by a mysterious figure can be traced back through the perfumer-glover's client list — a "follow the luxury good" investigation thread, mirroring the saddler's stitching-trace hook.
